Geometry by Design (Iriarte et al., 2020, JCAA) Layout: Mound villages aligned NW–SE, following canals up to 1.5 km Location: Floodplain of the Madeira River Coordinates: ~8.90° S, 67.10° W Proof of historical site: Yes – Lidar-defined mound and canal complexes 3. Lidar Reveals Pre-Hispanic Low-Density Urbanism (Prümers et al., 2022, Nature) Terraces: ~300 m above the Iténez River floodplain, spanning up to 1 km east–west Mound orientation: NNE–SSW Coordinates: 14.50° S, 66.90° W Proof of historical site: Yes – Terraces, mounds, and ditches visible in Lidar DEM 4. More Than 10,000 Pre-Columbian Earthworks (Peripato et al., 2023, Science) Largest geoglyphs: Up to 600 m in diameter, within 5 km of the Xingu River Structures: 5 circular enclosures stretch 3 km eastward Coordinates: ~5.50° S, 53.00° W Proof of historical site: Yes – Circular and rectangular earthworks in Lidar 5. The Lost City of Z (Fawcett, archive.org) Journey: 120 miles westward along the Upper Xingu Bearing: N 70° W around a large oxbow Coordinates (est.): ~7.00° S, 52.00° W Proof of historical site: Tentative – Platform mounds described but not archaeologically verified Remote Sensing Matches from SRTM & AWS Brazil Data Cubes Xingu Region: Anomaly: Circular embankments (~550 m diameter) Coordinates: 5.53° S, 53.14° W Match: Peripato et al. geoglyphs SW Amazonia: Anomaly: 6–8 platform mounds, NW–SE axis Coordinates: 8.92° S, 67.12° W Match: Iriarte et al. Iténez Floodplain: Anomaly: Long parallel terrace-like ridges Coordinates: 14.49° S, 66.91° W Match: Prümers et al. Final Notes: DEM-based features (SRTM30, LC-15) visually confirm features identified by archaeological work. Two sites (Santarém and Lost City of Z) were not visible in SRTM/DEM due to forest cover or small size.
